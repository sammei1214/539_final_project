Confirmation Bias

Confirmation bias is the tendency to search for, interpret, and recall information in a way that confirms our preexisting beliefs. This interactive demonstration will reveal how we naturally gravitate toward evidence that supports our initial assumptions.

Understanding Confirmation Bias

Most people tend to select evidence that aligns with their initial belief. This demonstrates how confirmation bias influences our information gathering and decision-making processes. In real life, this can prevent us from considering alternative viewpoints and making fully informed decisions.

Learn More

Confirmation bias affects how we consume news, evaluate political candidates, and make personal decisions. Being aware of this bias can help us actively seek out diverse perspectives and challenge our own assumptions.
