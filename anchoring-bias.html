Anchoring Bias

Anchoring bias occurs when we rely too heavily on the first piece of information we receive when making decisions. This initial information serves as an anchor that influences all subsequent judgments, even when the anchor is arbitrary or irrelevant.

Understanding Anchoring Bias

Research shows that people who see a higher initial number tend to give higher estimates, while those who see a lower number give lower estimates. The actual retail price of this keyboard is around $80. The initial question was designed to anchor your thinking, demonstrating how arbitrary information can influence our judgments.

Learn More

Anchoring bias affects negotiations, shopping decisions, and financial planning. Salespeople often use this bias by showing expensive items first, making subsequent items seem more reasonable. Awareness of anchoring can help you make more objective decisions by considering multiple reference points.
