Availability Bias

Availability bias is the tendency to overestimate the likelihood of events based on how easily examples come to mind. Recent, vivid, or emotionally charged events are more mentally available, leading us to believe they occur more frequently than they actually do.

Understanding Availability Bias

After reading news about flight incidents, most people overestimate the risk of air travel, even though statistically it remains one of the safest forms of transportation. The vivid headlines made flight risks more mentally available, influencing your perception. This demonstrates how media coverage and memorable events can distort our risk assessments.

In reality, you are far more likely to be injured in a car accident during your daily commute than in a plane crash, but car accidents receive less dramatic media coverage and feel more routine.

Learn More

Availability bias affects how we perceive risks in healthcare, finance, and daily life. News media often amplifies rare but dramatic events, making them seem more common than they are. Understanding this bias can help you make more rational decisions based on actual statistics rather than memorable examples.
